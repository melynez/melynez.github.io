About

Hi there, my name is Melyne! I’m a 16 y/o exploring all things bioelectricity and diverse intelligence. Currently, I'm working with Dr. Michael Levin investigating complexity algorithms. I’ve previously worked in labs from materials science to organoids for cancer (see Projects).

I’m interested in the psychology of unconventional systems—i.e. applying behavioral tests typically administered in mice to deceptively simple organisms, novel algorithms, and synthetic/bioengineered systems. I'm also fascinated by “code” in biology, or biological circuits of patterns of communication between cells in the brain and the body to understand learning and behavior.

I’m a fast learner and I love diving down rabbit holes. Recent curoisities include engrams as they relate to personality disorders and cool but weird organisms (Boquila trifoliata, the "mimicking" plant, and Tardigrades, extremophiles).
